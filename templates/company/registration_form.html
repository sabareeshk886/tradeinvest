GENERAL TERMS AND CONDITONS:

  1. Company registration: The company must be legally registered and recognized in its country of origin.

  2. Financial requirements: The company may need to meet specific financial requirements such as minimum revenue, profitability, and net worth.

  3. Disclosure requirements: The company must disclose information about its business, financial performance, and any legal or regulatory issues to the stock broker.

  4. Compliance with regulations: The company must comply with relevant regulations and rules set by the stock exchange, securities regulators, and other authorities.

  5. Shareholder requirements: The company may need to have a certain number of shareholders or a minimum percentage of shares that are publicly traded.

  6. Trading requirements: The company must agree to comply with the broker's trading rules and procedures.

  7. Fees and commissions: The company must agree to pay fees and commissions to the broker for its services.

  8. Risk disclosures: The company must acknowledge the risks involved in trading on the stock exchange and agree to assume responsibility for any losses incurred.

It is important for companies to carefully review the terms and conditions before entering into an agreement with a stock broker to ensure they are comfortable with the requirements and obligations.
